HydroShield Injector

Every year, there are ~297k new breast cancer diagnoses in the US, with 60% undergoing surgery. Percutaneous thermal ablation can destroy malignant tumors minimally-invasively with heat/cold. However, its application is limited: ablating too long risks sensistive tissue off-target damage; ablation too short risks tumor recurrence. HydroShield has developed a viscous hydrogel to protect off-target tissues during breast tumor thermal ablation that also reduces operating time. I am designing an injector medical device to output the gel at a controlled rate, in a sterile and ergonomic manner, while remaining affordable.

Zurich Artificial Heart

Heart failure (HF) affects over 23 million people globally, with 90% mortality after 10 years Suitable heart donors are rare and ex-vivo/synthetic options are only bridges-to-transplantation. Total artificial hearts (TAH) have also historically been limited by size and pneumatic drivelines. I helped develop a fully-implantable soft TAH using a hydraulically-actuated corrugated diaphragm to replicate pulsatile flow. I used FEA and CFD to evaluate diaphragm parameters' impact on volume displacement, von-Mises stress, and blood stagnation. Biomaterials were also selected to improve stress-strain functionality and enhance durability for future first-in-human trials.

Chemical Plant Capstone

Hatch Inc., an Toronto engineering consulting company, sponsored my bachelor's capstone. With battery, and electric vehicle production increasing exponentially, the company was interested in demonstrating battery-grade lithium carbonate (Li2CO3) production feasibility from concentrate extracted from hard-rock spodumene (80-96% wt Li2CO3) in Southern Ontario. My capstone team and I designed a pilot plant capable of producing 750 kg/day of battery-grade Li2CO3. The plant was to be fully assembled and operated operated within standard 40 ft ISO shipping containers, making it highly portable and compact for tradeshows.
